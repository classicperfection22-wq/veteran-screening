Eligibility Requirements — Please Read Before Applying

You must meet ALL three conditions below to qualify for VA disability compensation assistance.

🍂
Requirement 1 — Military Service
You must have served on active duty, active duty for training, or inactive duty training. National Guard and Reserve members may qualify if activated under federal orders.
🩹
Requirement 2 — Current Disability
You must have a current physical or mental health condition diagnosed by a medical professional. A past injury alone is not sufficient — a present diagnosis is required.
🔗
Requirement 3 — Service Connection
Your disability must be service-connected — caused, worsened, or aggravated by an event, injury, illness, or exposure that occurred during your military service.
📄
What to Have Ready
Your DD-214 (Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty). Medical records supporting your claim are strongly recommended but not required to submit this form.

We Cannot Assist If:

  • You received a Dishonorable Discharge (bars VA benefits in most cases)
  • Your condition existed prior to service and was not aggravated by service
  • You have no current diagnosis — past injuries alone are not sufficient
  • You were separated for misconduct without a discharge upgrade
